The fast spread of false information poses major obstacles in important areas
like politics, healthcare, and economics. To tackle these obstacles, explainable
fake news detection is seen as a vital remedy, not just focusing on determining
the truth of news but also on offering understandable justifications for these
determinations. This research focuses on utilizing Large Language Models
(LLMs) in conjunction with the ISOT Fake News Dataset to develop an
enhanced framework for detecting fake news while offering explanations.
Our system introduces two new techniques: LLM-GAN, a generative adversarial
method where the model adjusts to combat very tricky fake news created during
training, and LangGraph, a framework with a feedback loop that improves
detection strategies by studying previous misclassifications. LangGraph
combines natural language processing and adaptive knowledge graphs to
improve decision-making accuracy and reasoning as time goes on.
